# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, one surface. Readers who arrive from search or links to read 15+ years of essays on web development, philosophy, and craft — they want a beautiful, unhurried reading experience. And professional visitors (recruiters, collaborators, peers) for whom the site itself is the portfolio: the design is the proof of the "Fullstack Philosopher" claim.

## Product Purpose

The personal site of Jack Reichert — a design-led home for a 169-post writing archive synced from WordPress into a markdown static site (Eleventy). The goal reaction from a first-time visitor is "how was this made?" Success = the site reads as a design statement that happens to hold a deep archive, not a blog with a theme applied.

## Brand Personality

Bold, editorial, personal. The design is a revival of the 2020 jackreichert.com (archived: web.archive.org/web/20200919022300/https://www.jackreichert.com/), which Jack loved and left only because that stack lacked the WordPress backend — a constraint this rebuild removes. Cormorant Garamond + Montserrat thin/black, teal & vermillion, letterboxed photographs with overlapping white title cards, vertical rotated dates, doublebar rules. The voice is one person's — a craftsman who loves reading, design, and type. See DESIGN.md; identity-preservation governs all visual decisions.

## Anti-references

The user declined to name hard anti-references. Standing guardrails: nothing that reads as a generic blog theme or an AI-generated scaffold; magazine energy must not tip into clutter; book restraint must not tip into anonymity.

## Design Principles

1. **Two registers, one voice.** Browsing surfaces (home, archive) are magazine-bold and image-forward; reading surfaces (posts, pages) are book-calm and type-forward. The switch between them is the design's signature move.
2. **The photos are the color.** Ink-on-paper palette with a single decisive accent; Jack's own photography supplies all other color. Chrome never competes with imagery.
3. **Type is content.** Posts without a featured image get a typographic treatment that holds its own in the grid — type as imagery, never a gray placeholder.
4. **Practice what you preach.** A site by someone who writes about responsive design, font stacks, and typography must be flawless at every viewport — the archive's own essays are the acceptance criteria.
5. **Fifteen years is the feature.** Surfaces should convey the depth of the archive (dates, years, volume) as a point of pride, not bury it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Light theme only (deliberate, so the paper surface can be opinionated). WCAG AA baseline: ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast, visible focus states, keyboard-navigable, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation, semantic HTML throughout.
