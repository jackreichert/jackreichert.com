I’ve taken a step back from following all the podcasts I’d been listening to, for obvious reasons. I have the 3 I listen to, to keep up with the news… And that’s it. At a certain point, reading more news stories will only make me less effective.

In [2015 I set out to read a book a week](https://www.jackreichert.com/2015/12/my-2015-weekly-book-project/), and ended up reading 83 books. In order to keep my sanity, I plan to attempt this project again this year.

I’ve already started, and you can [follow my progress.](https://jackreichert.net/category/the-weekly-book-project/)

One thing I’d like to do differently this time around, as you might have noticed, is I want to absorb more of what I read. So I started writing a few paragraphs about each book, a takeaway, once I’m done.

I also wouldn’t mind company on this journey. I love discussing ideas, and what I’ve read and am thinking about. You can see from my [2015 list](https://www.jackreichert.com/2015/12/my-2015-weekly-book-project/) and my [most recent additions](https://jackreichert.net/category/the-weekly-book-project/) that it’s an eclectic collection. So if you’d like to suggest a book you’d like to discuss, or know what I’m reading before I’ve finished, [let me know](https://www.jackreichert.com/contact/) and I’d be more than happy to discuss.

For the curious, the secret to accomplishing this, is to plan. I always have several books in my queue that I’m in the middle of, and a few shorter books (Shakespeare plays, etc.) that I can finish in a day or so in case the end of the week surprises me. That way I’m always ready to finish a book each week.
